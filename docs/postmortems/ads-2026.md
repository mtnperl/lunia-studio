## What we tried (twice)

Paste a competitor ad in, have Claude rewrite the copy in Lunia voice, have fal.ai generate a matching 1:1 image. Output three angle variants per paste. Ship to Meta.

**Attempt 2 — Framework #4 (shipped 2026-04-14).**
Bigger swing. Brand colors locked, structured creative brief, a canvas compositor producing final static Meta ads with overlay text burned in. Recraft V4 for the hero image, Seedream edit loop for revisions. Brand asset library (product shots + logos) with auto bg-removal + auto-tag. Static ad concept generator prompting Opus 4.7 for 3 variants per angle (7 angles defined in `src/lib/ad-prompts.ts`).

## The bet

AI-assisted ad production would be faster and higher-quality than manual work. Concretely: Mathan writes a hook, the system produces a brand-safe static ad ready for Meta — minutes instead of hours, cheaper than an agency, on-brand by construction.

## What actually happened

Output didn't cross the "ship it" bar. The specific failure modes:

- **Image model drift from brand.** Recraft and Seedream produced technically on-prompt images that still read "stock wellness," not Lunia. Guardrails (`BRAND_VISUAL_GUARDRAILS` in `src/lib/ad-prompts.ts:141`) constrained palette and composition, but couldn't encode taste — the thing that makes an ad actually feel premium. Every generation needed a human eye, which collapsed the time win.
- **Compliance was load-bearing, and it worked.** The `lintAdConcept` regex catch (em dashes, "treat/cure/prevent", exclamation caps) did its job. That wasn't the problem. Copy passed lint and still wasn't good.
- **The copy was competent and forgettable.** Claude-generated primary text followed every structural rule (hook → proof → soft CTA, no drug claims, late product mention) and still sounded like AI. Ad copy lives or dies on a specific turn of phrase. The prompt couldn't produce that reliably.
- **No creative review loop.** We built generation without building the "is this any good?" step. No A/B hooks in-app, no historical ad performance data, no way to learn which angle was pulling. Just a generator that spat out candidates Mathan then judged alone.
- **Wrong bottleneck.** The actual bottleneck on Lunia ads wasn't "produce more static ads faster." It was **taste, creator sourcing, and knowing which angles convert.** Generation tooling doesn't touch any of those.

## What we learned

- **Generation without a performance loop is a demo, not a tool.** Before we build the third ad-adjacent feature, we need evidence of which ads actually moved revenue. Without that signal, we're generating into a void.
- **For a health brand, visual taste is the moat.** The edge isn't "can we produce an ad" — it's "does this ad feel like Lunia and not like a supplement dropshipper." Current image models don't clear that bar unattended.
- **The sheet Mathan already maintains is the real product surface.** The UGC tracking sheet ("Lunia ads content monitoring April") is where the actual work happens — briefs, costs, delivery status, what posted. That's where the leverage is. Build there.
- **Ship compliance scanning as a reusable library.** `lintAdConcept` regex worked; port it to `src/lib/compliance.ts` for the UGC tracker. Reuse, don't rewrite.

## Signal for the third attempt

Before building another ad tool, require these three things first:

1. **Attribution.** `postedUrl` per creator → Meta ad metrics → Shopify conversion data. Know which posted ad earned which revenue before generating replacements.
2. **Evidence the bottleneck is tooling, not taste.** If Mathan's best ad of the month took two hours to produce and pulled 3x ROAS, the bottleneck isn't "time to produce" — it's "time to figure out what to produce." Generators don't solve that.
3. **A review loop baked in from day one.** Not a generate button with no context. A workflow that learns from posted→earned outcomes.

The UGC tracker is step 1 of that list. Anything further on ad-generation tooling is gated on step 2 — specifically a 30-day period after UGC tracker ships where we look at what the data actually says.
